## The Big Picture

ASPA's revenue strategy has two engines:

**Engine 1 — STCH (Screenprinting, Transfer & Custom Hub):** A buying group / group purchasing program where ASPA negotiates exclusive pricing with suppliers, members buy through STCH at a discount, and ASPA earns 1-3% on every transaction. This is the primary, scalable revenue engine.

**Engine 2 — Sponsorship & Advertising:** Supplier partnerships that put brands in front of ASPA's audience through the website, newsletter, and events. This is the complementary, predictable revenue layer.

Both engines feed each other: sponsorship relationships become STCH supplier partners, and STCH transaction volume gives suppliers a reason to deepen their sponsorship investment.

## STCH: THE BUYING GROUP

### What STCH Is

STCH (Screenprinting, Transfer & Custom Hub) is ASPA's group purchasing program. It works like this:

1. **ASPA negotiates** exclusive pricing with suppliers on behalf of its members
2. **Members purchase** through STCH at discounted rates they couldn't get individually
3. **ASPA earns 1-3%** on every transaction as the facilitator/buying group
4. **Suppliers get** guaranteed volume and a direct channel to active screen printers

This is the same model used by group purchasing organizations (GPOs) in healthcare, hospitality, and other industries. The industry standard is that the GPO earns an administrative fee (1-3%) paid by the SUPPLIER, not the member. Members save money, suppliers get volume, ASPA earns revenue — everyone wins.

### Why STCH Works for ASPA

- **Members save more than their ASPA+ dues on the first order** — $49/year membership pays for itself immediately
- **Revenue scales with member activity** — more members buying = more revenue, without proportionally more work
- **Aligns ASPA's incentives with members** — ASPA earns when members get value
- **Suppliers are motivated** — guaranteed volume pipeline from verified screen printing professionals
- **No competing service exists** — there is no screen printing buying group in the market today
- **Differentiator vs. Printing United** — they run a trade show; ASPA runs the buying group. Completely different, complementary value.

### Transaction Model Options

There are three ways to execute STCH, from simplest to most sophisticated. **Start with Phase 1, evolve as volume justifies the investment.**

#### Phase 1: Referral/Coupon Model (Start Here)
**How it works:** ASPA negotiates exclusive discount codes or pricing tiers with suppliers. Members log into the ASPA website, access the STCH page, and get unique codes or affiliate links to use when ordering from suppliers directly.

**Pros:**
- Zero platform cost — just a webpage with codes/links
- No inventory, no fulfillment, no payment processing by ASPA
- Quick to launch — can be live within weeks of signing first supplier
- Low risk for both ASPA and suppliers (just a trial period)

**Cons:**
- Harder to track exact transaction volume (depends on supplier reporting)
- 1-2% referral rate more realistic (vs. 3% on a marketplace)
- Less control over the member experience
- Some suppliers may not have affiliate infrastructure

**Revenue potential:** 1-2% on tracked transactions, or negotiated flat monthly fee per supplier based on estimated volume

**Tech needed:** A password-protected STCH page on screen-printing.us, basic affiliate link tracking (could use simple UTM parameters or affiliate platform like ShareASale/Impact)

#### Phase 2: Negotiated Group Pricing Portal (Year 2)
**How it works:** ASPA negotiates fixed pricing tiers with suppliers. Members access a dedicated STCH portal that shows negotiated prices side-by-side with retail. Orders are still placed with suppliers, but through an ASPA-branded interface that tracks everything.

**Pros:**
- Better tracking and reporting
- Members see clear "you saved $X" messaging — reinforces ASPA+ value
- ASPA can show suppliers exact conversion data
- 2-3% administrative fee is standard for this model

**Cons:**
- Needs a basic web portal or integration with supplier ordering systems
- Some development cost (~$2K-10K depending on approach)
- Requires supplier onboarding and API/data-sharing agreements

**Tech needed:** Custom STCH portal page, supplier integration (API or manual), member authentication, order tracking

#### Phase 3: Full Marketplace (Year 3+)
**How it works:** Members browse and order through an ASPA-hosted storefront. ASPA processes orders and routes to suppliers (dropship model) or aggregates orders for bulk purchasing.

**Pros:**
- Maximum control and margin (3%+ realistic)
- Full data ownership — ASPA knows exactly what members buy
- Best member experience — one-stop shop
- Strongest negotiating position with suppliers

**Cons:**
- Significant development and operational cost
- Payment processing, customer service, returns handling
- Inventory risk if doing bulk purchasing (avoidable with dropship)
- Probably needs dedicated platform (Shopify Plus, custom build, or GPO software)

**Tech needed:** E-commerce platform, payment processing, supplier dropship integration, member accounts, order management

**Recommendation: Launch with Phase 1 immediately, build toward Phase 2 within 12 months if volume justifies it.**

### Two Distinct Revenue Approaches

The research revealed that supplier revenue breaks into two fundamentally different plays:

**Play 1: Passive Affiliate Links (Content-Driven, No Member Perk Required)**
These work when ASPA's content drives a purchase decision and the buyer clicks through naturally. No discount needed — the trust and recommendation is the value. Best for niche/industry-specific products where ASPA is genuinely guiding a decision.

Works for: Transfer Express (ShareASale), McLogan Supply (affiliate), niche screen printing supply retailers. Revenue estimate: $200-400/month as traffic grows.

**Play 2: Negotiated Member Perks (STCH-Driven, Requires Outreach)**
These are the flagship ASPA+ perks — where membership provides tangible savings members can't get on their own. This is the real value proposition for joining. Companies like Adobe, Shopify, FedEx, and QuickBooks won't drive affiliate clicks through ASPA unless there's a compelling member-only offer.

Works for: Shipping (FedEx/UPS association rate programs), software (group discounts), blank garments (negotiated pricing), business tools (organizational rates). Revenue: admin fees + the membership growth they drive.

**Critical insight:** Members won't sign up for Adobe through ASPA if they get the same price going direct. The affiliate model only works for business tools when paired with an exclusive ASPA member discount. For these categories, flip from "we earn commission" to "we negotiate pricing you can't get alone" — the affiliate commission becomes icing, not the cake.

### Competitive RFP Strategy

For commodity categories, ASPA should create competitive bidding situations — modeled after the OMNIA Partners approach (e.g., calling Hertz, Avis, and Enterprise in for presentations on the car rental RFP).

**RFP-ready categories (commodity score 4-5 out of 5):**

| Category | Commodity Score | Key RFP Candidates | Why It Works |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Blank Garments** | 5/5 | Gildan/Hanes, S&S Activewear, SanMar, Alphabroder, ShirtSpace | Same product from any distributor. Price + shipping speed are the differentiators. This is the car rental RFP equivalent. |
| **Emulsion & Chemistry** | 4/5 | Chromaline, Ulano, Easiway, Kor-Chem, Rhino | Consumable commodity — printers burn through chemicals regularly. Low brand loyalty. |
| **Plastisol Inks** | 3/5 | Avient (8 brands), ICC, Screen Print Direct, Nazdar | Somewhat commoditized. Focus on plastisol first (more interchangeable than water-based). |
| **Screens, Mesh & Frames** | 4/5 | Ryonet, GM Graphics, Victory Screen, SAATI, Sefar | Standard mesh counts are identical across suppliers. Perfect for group buy. |
| **DTF Transfers** | 4/5 | Custom-Transfers.com, Howard CT, Ninja Transfers, DTFSheet, Cheetah DTF | Fragmented market with price war active. Suppliers hungry for volume. |
| **Shipping** | 5/5 | FedEx, UPS | Pure commodity. Classic GPO play — aggregate volume for negotiated rates. |

**NOT recommended for RFP (relationship-driven, high switching cost):**
- Presses & Equipment — brand loyalty too high (M&R vs Workhorse is like choosing car brands)
- Software — high switching cost; partner/integrate instead
- Sublimation Equipment — brand-specific ecosystem lock-in (Epson vs Sawgrass)
- Embroidery Equipment — extreme brand loyalty; pursue dealer programs

**Blanks RFP approach (first priority):**
1. Survey ASPA members on monthly blank garment spend (volume data = leverage)
2. Invite 4-5 top distributors to present (Gildan, S&S, SanMar, Alphabroder, ShirtSpace)
3. Show aggregated ASPA membership volume + growth trajectory
4. Individual Q&A sessions with each distributor
5. Request proposals: pricing tier, shipping terms, net terms, admin fee structure
6. Select winner(s) and announce STCH blanks deal to membership

### Shipping Partnership Strategy — FedEx & UPS

**The play:** FedEx Advantage and UPS Savings programs are specifically built for associations and buying groups. Members get 20-45% off published rates; ASPA earns an admin fee for driving enrollment. This is the single most tangible ASPA+ perk — a shop shipping 20 packages/month saves $50-100/month, which more than covers the $49/year membership fee on day one.

**Approach:**
- Target FedEx Strategic Alliance / FedEx Advantage team (NOT the affiliate program — that's for bloggers)
- Simultaneously reach out to UPS Connect / UPS Savings Program
- Lead with addressable market size, not current volume — "thousands of small businesses" is a pipeline story
- Frame ASPA as FedEx's/UPS's sales channel into a niche B2B vertical they can't efficiently reach alone
- Lean on OMNIA Partners and Corcentric background to establish GPO credibility
- Send both outreach emails within the same week to create competitive tension

## STCH Supplier Pitch

The pitch to suppliers is different from the sponsorship pitch. With STCH, you're not selling advertising — you're offering a sales channel.

### Value Proposition to Suppliers

**"ASPA gives you a direct pipeline to verified screen printing professionals who are actively buying. No wasted marketing spend — every dollar goes toward actual purchasing customers."**

Key talking points:
- **Pre-qualified buyers:** ASPA members are working screen printers, not hobbyists browsing YouTube
- **No marketing cost:** ASPA handles member communication, education, and trust-building
- **Predictable volume:** Group purchasing means aggregated, predictable orders
- **Low risk:** Start with a trial period; if it works, formalize the arrangement
- **Industry exclusivity available:** Be the recommended [ink/blank/emulsion] supplier for ASPA members
- **The administrative fee (1-3%) is paid from the supplier's margin** — members see only their discount

### What ASPA Asks From Suppliers

1. **Exclusive ASPA member pricing** — a discount tier better than standard wholesale (even 5-10% improvement matters)
2. **ASPA administrative fee** — 1-3% of member transactions, paid by supplier to ASPA
3. **Basic reporting** — monthly transaction volume from ASPA member purchases (by code/link)
4. **Optional: promotional support** — willing to provide content, samples, or exclusive offers for ASPA members

### What Suppliers Get

1. **Access to ASPA's member base** — verified screen printing professionals in 100+ countries
2. **Featured listing on STCH** — "ASPA Recommended Supplier" designation
3. **Article mentions** — products naturally referenced in ASPA's 18 educational articles
4. **Newsletter placement** — regular features in ASPA's member communications
5. **CE credit co-creation** — webinars/content that earns ASPA CE credits (drives engagement)
6. **Data and insights** — what ASPA members are buying, trending products, purchasing patterns

## STCH vs. Printing United: The Differentiation

| | PRINTING United | ASPA / STCH |
|---|---|---|
| **Model** | Trade show + broad membership | Buying group + niche certification |
| **Focus** | ALL printing (commercial, packaging, wide-format, digital, garment, electronics) | Screen printing ONLY |
| **Revenue** | Expo exhibitor fees, membership dues | Transaction fees (STCH), membership dues, sponsorship |
| **Supplier access** | 3 days/year at expo ($10K-50K/booth) | 365 days/year through STCH ($0 upfront for suppliers) |
| **Member benefit** | Education, networking, expo access | Education, certification, buying power, savings |
| **Audience size** | Broad (thousands across all printing) | Deep (hundreds of verified screen printers) |
| **Certifications** | G7+, DCP, PDAA (none screen printing specific) | CSP — the ONLY screen printing craft certification |
| **Buying group** | No | Yes (STCH) |

**They don't compete — they complement.** ASPA owns the screen printing niche. PRINTING United owns the big tent. The STCH buying group is something Printing United doesn't offer and likely won't — it's too niche for their broad audience.

**Potential partnership:** ASPA members get discounted Printing United Expo access; PRINTING United points screen-printing-specific inquiries to ASPA.

## Key Principles

1. **STCH is the primary engine.** Sponsorship is the secondary engine. Revenue from helping members save money is more sustainable and scalable than revenue from selling ads.

2. **Member value drives everything.** If a supplier partnership doesn't save members money or improve their business, we don't do it. STCH credibility depends on members trusting that we negotiate FOR them, not against them.

3. **Supplier fees come from their margin, not member pricing.** The 1-3% administrative fee should always be invisible to members. Members see savings. Suppliers see volume. ASPA sees revenue.

4. **Start with consumables, scale to equipment.** Blank garments and ink are bought every week — that's where transaction volume lives. Equipment is less frequent but higher ticket — good for Phase 2+.

5. **Data is leverage.** Every transaction through STCH generates data about what members buy, how often, and how much. That data makes ASPA's negotiating position stronger with every passing quarter.

6. **Scarcity creates urgency.** "One preferred supplier per category" drives suppliers to commit early or lose the slot to a competitor.
